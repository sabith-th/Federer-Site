Bio

Roger Federer is a professional tennis player currently ranked 10 by the Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP). Federer has won an open era record 18 Men's Singles Grandslam titles and was ranked No.1 in the world for a record 302 weeks, including a record 237 consecutive weeks. Federer is considered the greatest tennis player of all time by players, commentators and fans all around the world.

Federer turned professional in 1998 and was continuously ranked in the top 10 from October 2002 to November 2016. After his victory over Rafael Nadal in the 2017 Australian Open Final, John McEnroe concluded that "Federer [had] cemented himself as the greatest player of all time".

Records

Roger Federer has won an open record 18 Men's singles grandslams. Federer holds the record for the most weeks at No.1 ranking (302 weeks), as well as most consecutive weeks as No.1 (237 weeks). He has won a record 7 Wimbledon, 5 US Open, and 6 World Tour Finals (previously Masters Cup) including record 5 consecutive titles at both Wimbledon and US Open.
